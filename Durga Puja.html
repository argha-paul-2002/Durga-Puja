The Durga Puja is an annual festival of Hindus which is celebrated by worshiping Goddess Durga. Hindu Goddess Durga is belived to have the demon Mahishasura.The festival marks "victory of good over evil".It takes place in the month of Ashwin from the first of the tenth day of the bright fortnight. It is an occasion of great enthusiasm and festivity for the Hindus.On this occasion schools, collages, and offices are closed for a long holiday.People return their homes for celebrating the Puja. It is celebrated by the Hindus all over India and abroad.It is the most popular festival in the state West Bengal,India.

Special Features:

The Vijaya Dashami is considered to be an auspicious day. On the day people visit their relatives. The people of the treading classes being their new year from this day. At some place Ramlila is staged and Ravan is killed on the Dashami day.

Stories Connected with Durga Puja:

There are various stories connected with thw Vijaya Dashami. It is said that Ram killed Ravan on this day and performed the Puja of Goddess Durga. Since then it has been celebrated every year on this day. Another story is that one King Suratha performed the Puja on this day. The third is that Goddess Durga killed Mahishasura, a demon, on this day.
